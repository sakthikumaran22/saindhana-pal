Go Green Gardening!

Why Try Gardening?

  • It’s easy to do. Planting seeds and watching them grow is something anyone can do.

  • You don’t need lots of space. Having a huge garden is great, but you can grow all kinds of plants in pots or window boxes. Or how about keeping indoor plants on a windowsill?

  • It looks good. Masses of colorful flowers can really brighten up your day and they keep bees and other insects, happy too.

  • And tastes great. There’s nothing like munching homegrown fruits, vegetables, and herbs!

Tools


After you’ve decided which plants to plant, you need to gather some tools and prepare your garden space.

Gather the following tools:

  • Hoe - This garden tool has a thin, flat blade that is used for breaking up soil and removing weeds.

  • Garden Rake - This tool has a long handle with a row of teeth or prongs at one end. It is used to smooth the soil surface.

  • Measuring Tools - A yardstick and measuring tape can determine the proper distance between rows in your garden

  • Hand Trowel/Shovel - These tools are used to make shallow rows in which the seeds can be planted.

  • Watering Can or Hose - These are necessary to provide your plants with the water they need to grow.

  • Stakes and String - You will used these tools to mark the rows in your garden.

Digging In & Preparing The Soil:

Before planting, it’s important to prepare your soil.

  • If your soil is muddy, give it a few days to dry out. If it’s hard or dusty, water it thoroughly and then wait for a few days until it is damp throughout.

  • Next, pull out the weeds and cover your bed of soil with a layer of about 2-3 inches of finished, finely sifted compost. You can buy compost from your local garden nursery.

  • Use a fork or trowel to mix in the compost, loosen the soil and break up dirt clods. Finally, use a rake to mound up the soil and even out the top.

Starting From Seeds:

After buying the good quality seeds from a local nursery, read the package the seeds came in. It will say how much water and sunlight the seeds need.

  • Fill a biodegradable container with soil. Pack down the soil with your hands. Add water to the soil.

  • Place a seed in the soil and cover it up. Follow the instructions on the package.

  • Watch for you plant to sprout. Once it germinates, you can plant it in a bigger pot.

  • After your seed sprouts, it needs more space. It’s roots need room to grow. Here’s how to replant your seedling.
    • Water the seedling 1 hour before replanting it.

    • Fill a pot with soil and dig a hole in the soil.

    • Place the biodegradable container in the hole.

    • Cover it with soil and make sure the seedlings sticks out of the soil.

    • Make sure the roots do not touch the sides of the pot.

    • Water the plant well. Some pots have holes in bottom.

    • This helps drain the extra water. They should be placed on saucers to retain the water.


Color Your Garden:

After buying the good quality seeds from a local nursery, read the package the seeds came in. It will say how much water and sunlight the seeds need.

  • Flowers add color to a garden. They smell good, too! First, decide what you want to plant. Some flowers need sun and others need shade. Find a space in your garden where your flowers will get the light they need.

  • Some flowers can be bought in pots and replanted. Others can be grown by yourself from seeds or bulbs. Plant hardy bulbs in the fall. These bulbs need cold weather to grow in the spring. Plant tender bulbs in the spring and watch them flower in summer.

  • Annuals are the plants that need to be planted every year. They last for one growing season. They include Zinnia and many Sunflowers.

  • Some flowers are Perennials. They will flower for many growing seasons. Lilies and Tulips are perennials. They grow from bulbs.

Kitchen Gardening:

A kitchen garden is a garden that you grow indoors. It has edible or sweet smelling plants. Choose a few herbs to add to your kitchen garden. Indoor herbs need a safe place. Follow these rules to choose the best area in your home.

  • Make sure they won’t get knocked over or stepped on.

  • Put them where the temperature stays about the same.

  • Make sure the plants get sunlight, you can put them near a window.

  • Set a watering schedule. Check the soil to see if your plants need water. Stick a finger into the soil 1/2 inch, if it is dry, the plant may need water.

  • Light is very important for the indoor plants. To get the right light, know what direction the windows face. South facing windows give the most light. East and West facing windows have a medium amount of light. North facing windows have the least light.
